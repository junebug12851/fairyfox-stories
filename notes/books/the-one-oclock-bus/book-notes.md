## Form fit

Novella = one central conflict + one meaningful change, driven deep. The conflict: the audit vs.
the hour (the public record vs. the true one). The change: Abel from forger-for-good-reasons to a
man who hands over the true record and lets himself be delivered. No B-plot — Vera's lit window and
Priya's apprenticeship are both hooked to the main line (what a record is for; who gets the cab).
Ten chapters, section breaks inside chapters as needed.

## Distinctness check (vs. the shelf)

- Vs. the parent (*Launderette*, Posy Fell, cosy short story): different writer, different form,
  different engine — an institutional procedural squeezing a secret vs. one night's gentle offer.
  Kinship is thematic only (the realm's own law: you have to let it help you), which is what makes
  it a Hollow Hours book rather than a re-skin.
- Vs. the realm's other novella (*Every Lock but Hers*, Del Marsh, hard-boiled locksmith): that one
  is night-city noir about locks and refusal from the trade side; this is cool-literary about
  records and audit from the institutional side. Different voice entirely, different shape
  (investigation-closing-in vs. jobs-and-debts).
- Vs. Idris's own *Cartographer of Decks* (generation-ship SF): same authorial instrument — clean
  glass, the careful person noticing seams — pointed at a bus depot instead of a starship.
  Different genre, register, stakes, and warmth; the ending is a front door, not an airlock.
- Vs. the rest of the shelf: no gothic house, no caper, no carnival, no desert fable. The engine
  (an audit as antagonist-clock) is new on the shelf.
- Grows a codex seed almost verbatim: "a night bus driver who knows where everyone needs to go but
  not where he does."

## Author-persona (the authoring lens — Idris Okonkwo-Vance, second book)

Plain / cool-literary: precise, restrained, observational; the prose gets out of the way; the
emotion arrives late and hits harder for it. For this book the instrument plays *warmer* material
than *Cartographer* — the trick is to let Abel's procedural devotion carry the feeling: he measures
what he cannot say. Signature moves to keep: the load-bearing detail (timings, fares, fuel);
institutions and the myths they run on (the timetable as covenant; the hearing that files a miracle
under *demand-responsive*); the seam noticed quietly and reported flatly. Blind spot to guard:
Idris distrusts grand feeling — fine — but this realm is *kind*; do not let cool become cold. The
hour is shy and warm; render it through what it does, never explain it.

## Voice & POV

First person, past tense, **Abel Mensah**. He narrates like a man completing a log entry he knows
will be read: exact times, exact places, no adjectives he can't defend. The daylight between
account and truth: he is perfectly reliable about the route and quietly unreliable about *why* he
keeps it — Corvus Road appears in his narration only as a timing point until ch.5 forces it open.
Guard against: I-stacking (open on the road, the mirrors, the doors); naming feelings (he checks
gauges when moved); Sela's voice (she was educated-formal and elegiac; Abel is trade-devout and
dry — a different cool).

## Style dials (style-spectrum)

Diction plain, concrete, trade-inflected (headways, timing points, duty cards). Syntax short-to-
medium declaratives; the rare long sentence reserved for the hour itself. Imagery sparse and
selective — fluorescent, rain, the green glow of the dash; one image per scene carries it.
Figuration near-none; when it comes, it's a driver's (the city "idling", a street "in gear").
Humor dry, buried in the procedure. White space: section breaks at shift-points of the night.
