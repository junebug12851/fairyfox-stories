# Book-notes — The Girl Who Sold the Wind (internal)

- **Author-persona:** Amara Okri — exuberant fabulist. **Style:** fable/folkloric, told-aloud;
  pattern of threes, turned phrases, sly asides, a sideways moral. Bright, never grim.
- **POV:** first person past, Zari (14); boastful, funny, addresses the reader; learns hard & sweet.
- **Continuity:** the Salt Road across the Bright Waste; Ninth Waystation. Winds are real, bottled,
  sold (dawn/grief/homesick); homesick wind = heaviest, costliest, carries you home once. Rule:
  "a wind, once sold, cannot be recalled." Ch.1: Zari sells the homesick wind to a veiled stranger
  (triple pay, no haggle) who is herself an **exiled wind** (a drift, not a face) — means to blow
  "home" into the city that cast her out and drown it in longing.
- **Arc:** wants "enough" (coin/security/stall); needs to learn cleverness-without-care = greed, and
  "enough is not a number." Ch.4 the third-bargain turn (gives up what she wants most); Ch.5 she
  buys the exiled wind a true way home by understanding it — poorer, larger.
- **Status:** ch.1 of 5 written.
