### The chapter

**Ch.4 "The Understudy" (~2,600 words)** — the novelette's midpoint turn, and the **complicity beat**
the whole book is built toward (`character.md`; blueprint arc §). The design constraint the plan set
back in ch.3 was the hard one: **ch.4 adds no new clue.** Sonora has had every fact since the third
morning (spare-hasp relock · kerosene hide · bills-kept/coin-buried · the doorway). So the chapter
could not be a discovery; it had to be the *courage to stop crediting the alibi she wrote for the
girl* — detective certainty as a moral act, not an evidentiary one. Craft decisions worth recording:

- **Opening doctrine held (the ch.4–6 pattern).** Ch.1 opened on the trick, ch.2 on cold-vs-hot, ch.3
  on the tell; ch.4 opens on **the understudy** — every act has one, a show that stops starves, so
  nothing is allowed to be the only one of itself, and you teach your replacement *on purpose* ("a
  professional would sooner be replaced well than mourned badly"). Turned home in the same breath: the
  finest instrument the machine ever grew "trained her own replacement and taught the girl, first
  week, how to lock a door from the outside." Lark is Sonora's understudy, and she learned the act
  too well.
- **Reconciling ch.3's Bruno-clearing with the blueprint's "let Bruno hang" — the split-crime pivot.**
  This was the run's real puzzle. Ch.3 came off Bruno for the *robbery* (the coin stayed on the lot,
  killing the drifter-robs-and-runs story), but the blueprint's monstrous-comfortable option is
  explicitly *let Bruno hang, keep the girl*. Resolved by giving the tired sheriff a **two-stories**
  pivot under Friday pressure: an inside carny was light-fingered with the take (ugly, settled quiet,
  no rope), and **the Colonel's death** — the thing the town wants a rope for — gets set back on the
  outsider. Dell aims it without a word (sad-about-outsiders patter over the fourth supper; offers
  Bruno's never-seen temper as proof of the temper). Keeps ch.3 fully intact **and** honours the
  blueprint. **The ch.3 plant pays here** exactly as flagged ("Cleared is not brought back … you will
  want to remember that in three chapters" → collected: "a man already outside the circle is halfway
  to the tree").
- **The option priced at nothing, requiring no act — the flaw continued, not a new deed.** The horror
  of the beat is that the monstrous thing is a *continuation of Sonora's defining silence*, not a
  fresh crime: she need only keep the spare-hasp gaff she's held since ch.1, and Bruno hangs, the show
  rolls whole, and she keeps Lark alive. "Not a new sin … the same old silence, kept one week longer
  … I had already paid for it in advance, fifteen years ago." The reward is named and real (the wall,
  the blindfold, the girl), so the temptation is honest and not strawmanned.
- **Fair-play reinforced at the roped-off pay-wagon.** She re-reads the padlock: the Colonel was a
  *jammer* ("a lock the Colonel set looked angry"); this one was set soft, the spare turn taken — her
  own taught courtesy (*"close it like you're apologizing to it, child"*). Taught only to a short roll
  of trusted hands, of whom exactly one has "light feet, a bruise she wouldn't name, no road behind
  her." Ch.3's "half the lot, I told myself" is now openly named as the lie she knew it was.
- **Lark's SECOND tell — the absence of the first.** New, and I think the chapter's best move: on the
  first morning (ch.2) her hands stopped and she looked at the stove (a frightened child, wall still
  going up). Four days later Sonora drops the Colonel's name near her *on purpose* and her hands do
  **not** stop — the calm is now flawless. The one slip only the teacher can read is that **her breath
  does not change at all**, and "the only people who manage their breath are people who have been
  taught to, and I taught her that too." The tell is that the tell is gone: the understudy has
  finished learning the act. Heartbreak-only evidence — reads solely to Sonora, proves nothing to a
  court, which keeps the vise moral rather than procedural.
- **Refusal is NOT virtue (guarded hard, per the flaw).** Explicit anti-nobility in-text: "I have
  looked for my virtue in that moment for the rest of my life and it is not there." What saves Bruno
  is the same flaw that doomed her — she cannot leave a face unread; to take the free way out she'd
  have to be "the fraud I have always let people think I am … and the whole cursed engine of me is
  that I am not a fraud, I only wish I were." She can keep the gaff from a tired sheriff; she cannot
  keep it from Lark or from herself. She goes to the girl **with no plan and no nobility** — the cold
  reader's only compulsion — which sets up ch.5 without pre-spending its confrontation.
- **Lark on-page as ch.3 promised** ("Ch.4 reopens on her"): at the cook-house cutting bread (her
  large-world reflex — a task with an end). Observed only; **the mechanics of the night stay unspoken**
  — that's ch.5's work. Ends on the doorway rhyme (Sonora summons her from *a doorway*, "the same
  shape of frame she had stood in offering me all of this for free"), the knife going "still the way a
  clock goes still, all at once and on purpose," and Bruno at the tent flap "watching his own trial
  walk away into the dark to make up its mind."
- **Restraint / ration held.** Death stays off-page and un-lurid; Lark's fear and bad-home past are
  non-explicit. Patter runs, then the bare lines land ("It hurt." / "On Bruno." / "It hurt."). Ada
  Prewitt deliberately **not** used — saving the name-coming-off-the-act for ch.6.
