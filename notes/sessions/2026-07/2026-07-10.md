**The book.** **Every Lock but Hers** — magical-realism **novella**, universe **the Hollow Hours**,
author **Del Marsh**. The craft reasoning: the join+reuse rolls could have produced a re-skin, so I
picked the *least* re-skin-prone combination — Del Marsh's hard-boiled-tender caper voice pointed at
the Hollow Hours' gentle magic. It's unlike Posy Fell's whimsical *Two-O'Clock Launderette* short
(the only other Hollow Hours book) and unlike Marsh's own Gaslight-Reach caper, and it takes the
thinnest universe from one small short to a tonally different pair. Demonstrates the mesh: an author
spanning universes; a realm deepening.

**Premise / engine.** Nell Corrigan, night locksmith, can open any door in the city and has bolted
the one she shut on her brother Theo three years ago. In the hollow hour, a lock opens onto the
places she's refused to go — the price is one true thing she's never said aloud, and once said it's
spent. One conflict, one change (locking-out → letting-in): a novella's concentration. Eleven
chapters; the whole arc, the magic's exact rule, and who's behind the last door are in the public
`blueprint.md`. **Ch.1 *The Two O'Clock Call*** written in full to prove the voice (a routine
lock-out; the first faint hollow-hour wrongness — a door opening onto her own old kitchen; Mar's
closer/eviction call and the debt). Ch. 2–11 left for the daily grow job.

**Scaffolded / wired.** `_books/every-lock-but-hers.md`, `stories/every-lock-but-hers/blueprint.md`,
`_chapters/every-lock-but-hers/01.md`, `_characters/{nell,theo}-corrigan.md`. Shelf auto-lists from
the `books` collection (no README/universe-file edit needed — README carries no per-book list; the
universe file is a codex, not a book index). Cover: designed title-fallback (art deferred,

- **The Wintering House — ch.2 *The Small Parlour*** (Cordelia Fenn, gothic novella). Supper in
  the one warm room; introduces Mr Ravenscar (grave, courteous, ~45) and his sister Miss Charlotte
  Ravenscar (bright, brittle) — the late matriarch Mrs Ravenscar is their mother, the "mistress"
