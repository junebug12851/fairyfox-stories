**Written.**

- **The Wintering House — ch.2 *The Small Parlour*** (Cordelia Fenn, gothic novella). Supper in
  the one warm room; introduces Mr Ravenscar (grave, courteous, ~45) and his sister Miss Charlotte
  Ravenscar (bright, brittle) — the late matriarch Mrs Ravenscar is their mother, the "mistress"
  Agnes is nominally companion to. Plants the secret hard: a fourth place laid, served, and cleared
  untouched; Agnes says the name and the room stills; she's asked to help "keep her room" warm, and
  — hungry to belong — folds away the cold clear part of herself and agrees, asking nothing. Ends on
  the tended fire behind the closed door she tells herself is the wind. Opens the "Comfortable Lie"
  movement (ch 2–4).
- **The Girl Who Sold the Wind — ch.2 *The First Day's Chase*** (Amara Okri, fable). Zari corks her
  stall and chases the grey stranger east. Fable hook: the three wind-metal coins can't buy passage
  (the caravan master recoils — seeded for ch3's reveal). She cheats the water-boy **Rafiq** out of
  his bonded caravan ticket with a false dying-grandmother story; he won't shake loose, walking at
  the tail of his own caravan. First evidence of the loosed homesick wind: a caravan halted in the
  open Waste, everyone sitting weeping-with-longing toward home. Zari gets down and starts walking.
- **The Cartographer of Decks — ch.2 *Deck Nought*** (Idris Okonkwo-Vance, SF). Sela returns alone,
  opens the sealed hatch (manual latch on the inside face — someone wanted to open it), and finds a
  founders' observation deck with a 20-metre window: the star-field is dead-still — no drift, so no
  drive. The velocity log dates the engines going dark to 91 years ago; a founders'-hand projection
  reads "10,000 years — if ever." The Purpose is a kindness and a lie. Ends on the chilling seam
  into ch.3: one console wiped clean, one chair worn free of dust, a fresh two-foot track to the
  hatch — someone from the Office still comes here.
