### The chapter

**Ch.8 "A Warm Death" (~3,100 words)** — "The Secret" (ch.8–9) opens. The blueprint's brief: Agnes
learns the full shape of what the family keeps warm. Ch.7 had already given her the *facts* by
theft (the living hand, the name Ada, the E.M. workbox); so ch.8's job was the truth at the next two
scales — the **world's complicity** and the **second swallowed grief** — and the craft decisions
worth recording:

- **The rite before the reasons.** The New Year reading (planted ch.6) staged as the house's one
  ceremony: once-a-year fires lit, procession, two chairs inside the door and no furniture moved,
  and Marchbank reading **the eleventh sermon — against immoderate grief — from the page as it
  lies, hands behind his back, never touching the book**. It's the sermon *she was reading*; the
  browned shape (ch.3's plant) sits under his eyes as he reads; he smooths the corner and puts the
  spectacles back. Twelve years of the one sermon the household cannot hear, read to the one member
  of it who cannot hear anything. "A happy New Year to you, ma'am," to the bed.
- **The world indicted gently, in its own voice.** Marchbank on the walk down gets the book's
  fairest self-accusation — a shepherd goes where the flock is; "visiting the sick, or sitting down
  to table with the sickness"; and the thesis in a farmer's image: *grief is a river — dam it, and
  it floods the fields you live from.* He also pays the moss-plant forward without closing it: not
  him; dark hours between a Saturday and a Sunday; **the beck path keeps a frost footprint**. Ch.9
  opens with Agnes at her window on the Saturday night.
- **Ada resolved — and the resolution deepens the disease instead of explaining it away.** Ada was
  **Mrs Hale's daughter**: born in the laundry-loft, always cold, nineteen when she was taken
  upstairs for the first keeping, dead of a chest fever in the third winter — **eleven nights her
  mother asked for her to be moved into the warm room; "it was never once said no … it was only
  never done."** The genre bar held exactly as `craft/genre-range.md` asks: the horror is
  arithmetic (thirty feet, eleven nights, north wall where the wind is), nothing depicted. And the
  reveal re-reads two shipped beats honestly: Charlotte's ch.7 flinch is a **kept promise** (Mrs
  Hale raked out the laundry-loft fire Charlotte had begun laying and took her word never to say
  the name — "I would not have my girl made a *she*; I'd sooner she were cold and mine than warm
  and theirs"), and Mrs Hale's ch.4/ch.5 "warm death" lines now have their bill attached.
- **The chapter's total is Agnes's arithmetic, not an event:** the advertisement wanted "a woman
  the world would not miss" — E.M. had a *Dearest Mother* to write to and her sentence still never
  got out; Agnes has no letter anyone is waiting for. Chosen *because* she has nobody. "Keep your
  money, Miss Thorne" is the first exit anyone has offered her — placed one chapter before the
  family's Offer (ch.10) prices staying.
- **Open-thread discipline:** where the companions went stays unanswered (Mrs Hale refuses the
  E.M. question; "they went"). Not a murder story; the dread is the un-asking.
