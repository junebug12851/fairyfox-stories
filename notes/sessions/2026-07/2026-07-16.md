## Written — one whole chapter each

- **Every Lock but Hers — ch.4 "The Bolt"** (~1,600 words; blueprint ch.4). The wound named, in Nell's
  voice rather than through the magic: Theo the warm younger brother "born without a door"; the rainy
  night three years ago he knocked once and she threw the bolt because the people he owed were out in the
  wet; the sweet-cut lie *he left* she's re-cut ever since — a lie that came true in the one direction
  she can't stand (she genuinely can't find him now). The hollow-hour test lands in the parked van, van
  key in hand, the true thing behind her teeth; she turns the ignition like anybody, pays nothing, gets
  nothing, and **does not say it**. Reuses ch.2's rain/one-knock furniture as designed, spends none of
  ch.9's full payoff. Trade-lore = the over-lifted lever that binds as hard as an unlifted one.
- **The Cinderwick Job — ch.5 "The Auction"** (~1,840 words; blueprint ch.5). The heist under the night
  auction's roar. North-wall passage dry-set and opened for them; the lift off the iron perch beautiful.
  Then the Nightingale sings **unbidden** — Crane's own unhurried voice arranging the trap ("print their
  numbers on one sheet… I only want them holding it when the room closes"), proving the score was bait
  and Juno the catch. Under it, from forty years down, the bird riffles one older voice — *Wren, Junia…
  deliver her Tuesday* — the man who sold her, the private motive she'd denied wanting (nearly buckles
  her; **not** resolved — ch.7 wins by trusting a person, not by hearing that voice). The passage goes
  dark behind them (Crane's two domestic sounds, now real); cornered, Juno decides to bet on Kit's fear
  — "I'm going to tell you the truth about the fourth thing" — teeing up ch.6's double-cross.
- **The Wintering House — ch.6 "The Woman in the Ground"** (~1,915 words; blueprint ch.5–7, "The
  Cracks"). Agnes walks three miles to Fellgate church on Christmas; the family stays home keeping *her*.
  Out of the house's reach for the first time, she weeps with the plain relief of a kept creature let
  into the cold. The parish pities her like the bereaved (they've worn the thing smooth; the poor
  declining to gossip about the rich = the thing is past gossip). In the churchyard, the grave:
  *HONORIA RAVENSCAR — Wife of Aldous… She is not here* — twelve years dead, the Christian name the house
  never speaks given only to the ground, the moss kept clear of the letters by a faithful hand. An old
  woman, graveside-plain: *"There've been three or four of you… We don't see them come down."* Death
  confirmed, the fresh-ink letter of ch.5 now unbearable; Agnes knows she'll climb back and change the
  water anyway — and that the doing is the horror. The companions' fate stays **deliberately
  unanswered** (bible: the book is not a murder story). Introduces the mistress's Christian name
  (Honoria) canonically, from the stone.
