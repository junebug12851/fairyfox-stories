### The chapter

**Ch.2 "The Auditor" (~2,450 words)**, straight to the blueprint's brief: Vera in daylight, her
method and creed at full weight; Priya assigned; two watchers by the close. Craft decisions worth
recording:

- **The opposition is armed before it's argued with.** Vera's evidence is deliberately *good* —
  three independent telemetry series that agree (odometer vs schedule, fuel vs distance, door-cycle
  counts vs recorded boardings), giving 11.4 miles four nights in five and 12–16 on the fifth. She
  praises Abel's log sincerely, then declines to call it a compliment ("it was a finding"). If the
  reader can already see how she's wrong, the book has cheated; here she is simply *right about
  records*.
- **Her creed is paid for with a case, not asserted.** Care transport, nine years ago, a fudged
  *rota only* — everyone kind, the fudge keeping a needed service alive — a nineteen-hour stretch
  logged as eleven, a wall, a woman of eighty-one. Told in four sentences, nothing depicted, the
  genre bar (`craft/genre-range.md`) held: the weight is in the arithmetic, not the injury. It earns
  the line the whole audit thread hangs on: *a public record is what people with no power have
  instead of power … the somebody else is never in the room.*
- **The paid turn is the flaw at small scale, twice.** First the amnesty declined ("The route runs
  as printed") — and she writes down the answer *and the pause*, which is the chapter's quietest
  threat. Then the real cost: she has found the **01:26 Corvus Road crawl** herself, calls it the
  most reliable thing on the route, and offers **helpfully** to make it a timing point in the
  recommendations — and Abel says *"No. Nothing there."* His first outright lie in the book is told
  in daylight, on the record, to a fair person, and it is **not about the hour**. Eleven years of
  forgery protected the bus; the one thing he lied *aloud* about is his daughter's street. Ch.5 and
  ch.8 collect on that.
- **Daylight as the alien country.** The voice note says Abel measures what he cannot say, so the
  chapter's one carrying image is a **clock that is simply right** — Marchgate's lobby clock set
  against the depot's ninety-seconds-fast fuel island from ch.1. A man who believes clocks are
  "near enough, agreed upon, allowed their minutes" meets one that isn't allowed anything, and
  registers it as a detail rather than a feeling. No emotion named anywhere in the chapter.
- **Priya lands in eleven lines and stays a foil, not a device.** Chips at half four, feet on the
  bulkhead, apologising in triplicate — but she *reads* the cab (the third hook, the log rack, the
  blue notebook's corner under the duty cards), which makes ch.3's find honest rather than
  convenient. Her question here is deliberately the **smaller cousin**: why stand at the coach
  station stop at one in the morning. The Corvus question is held for ch.3.
- **Plants laid flat, not flagged:** Vera **doesn't drive** (she is a passenger — which is why the
  hour can reach her in ch.6/9); ride-alongs set at **three scheduled + one unannounced** (ch.7's
  second inspection ride now has its slot); the withdrawal stakes named in her own mouth ("there's
  no budget line for corrected"). The blue-book aphorism stays unspent for ch.8–9.
