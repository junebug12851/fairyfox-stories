## 0.3.4 — Grow: Wintering House, Cartographer, and Cinderwick each gain a chapter

A pure grow day — no plant (next eligible 2026-07-13), no sequel roll (nothing complete yet). Blend
took the top two; the random-override roll fired on the third slot (rolls `43, 94, 11`; ≤18 fires)
and drew *The Cinderwick Job* over the blend's *Girl Who Sold the Wind*.

- **The Wintering House — ch.3 "The Keeping of the Room."** Agnes is given the keeping of the warm
  room, and its rules arrive as ordinary housekeeping: the fire never out, the water changed though
  it's never drunk, the book never closed. Then Charlotte says *good morning, Mama* to a turned-down
  bed — easily, lovingly, not madly — and Agnes, starved of exactly that, folds the cold clear part
  of herself away and says good morning too. She notices the hair that comes out of the brush like
  thread and the page sun-browned from lying open under the same window for years, and she talks
  herself out of both. The best month she's had in ten years.
- **The Cartographer of Decks — ch.3 "The Office Knows."** Sela files the true number and is summoned
  by **Master Cartographer Ilkka Vosse**, who has been walking down to Deck Nought once a month for
  forty-one years. He shows her the drawer: thirty-one filings saying *161 m, confirmed* — and,
  beneath them, eleven more across four centuries, each logging 169 with a hatch drawn in, the one
  before his in her **grandmother's** hand. His defence is a cartographer's: *a map is a choice about
  what to draw, made by a person, for a person.* He gives her the key and a month to answer him. It's
  warm from his pocket, and what she feels is *chosen*.
- **The Cinderwick Job — ch.3 "The Cage."** Juno cases a building with no lazy man in it, then walks
  the viewing as an agent for a collector who has been dead six years. **Magister Crane** knows all
  of it, kindly, and admits the card and the client were both his — then presses bidder's chit 214
  into her hand and invites her to rob nothing and be hired instead. She leaves with the one thing he
  didn't hand her: while three hundred people applauded the Nightingale, Crane stood in his doorway
  and *listened* to it like a man waiting to hear his own name.

Also: the Wintering House housekeeper is renamed **Mrs Hale** in ch.1 (she collided with Aldous
Ravenscar), Sela's character page is corrected to thirty-one to match the prose, and Ilkka Vosse gets
a character page in the mesh. `npm test` green (5/5).

## 0.3.3 — Grow: three books advance a chapter each

A pure grow day — no plant (next eligible 2026-07-13). Picked by the least-tended-first blend; the
random-override roll came up 50/100, so no override fired and the top three by score were grown:

- **The Cinderwick Job — ch.2 "The Commission."** Juno asks at the Cage's consignment window and
  meets **Pell**, the go-between: 400 guineas to lift the **Cinderwick Nightingale** out of the Cage
  on auction night. She names the trap out loud and refuses — then Pell lays down a Cage ledger page
  from eleven years ago reading *"Wren, Junia. Delivered."* The bird has sat in that vault forty
  years, so it still holds the voice of whoever said her name to collect. She takes the job, and is
  honest with the reader that the leash isn't why.
- **The Two-O'Clock Launderette — ch.2 "What Comes Out."** Machine 7 won't stop; Nadia's coat comes
  out warm and dry, and her pockets — laid on the folding table — turn out to hold nothing that
  belongs to her. Her mother's order of service survives the wash perfect and *weightless*: the
  hollow hour rinses out the not-looking, not the grief. Then she puts it all back, item by item, out
  of habit — and is still holding her father's number when the hour ends.
- **Every Lock but Hers — ch.2 "The Closer."** Nell does the eviction lock-change on Dolvin Road (£60
  to open a door, £240 to close one — the same hands, the same hour) and watches the tenant walk up
  to it in her mirror without stopping. Two nights later, cutting the landlord's spares past 1 a.m.,
  she says the truest thing she's said in three years — *"I hate the closers"* — and a cupboard opens
  onto the landing outside her own old door, in the rain, with one knock. She works out the rule,
  refuses it, and stays clear of the hollow hour for eleven days.

## 0.3.2 — Shelf cards: loglines tuck behind a "?" corner tooltip

Adopted the fairyfox hub's project-card pattern on the story shelf: each book's logline no longer sits
as a always-on paragraph in the card body — it moves into a small **"?"** control in the cover's
top-right corner that reveals the line as a hover/focus tooltip. Cards read cleaner and scan faster
(cover, title, byline, progress — the pitch on demand), and the shelf now matches the hub's chrome
one more notch. Mechanics mirror the hub verbatim (`.proj-help` / `.proj-help-mark` / `.proj-tip`,
`cursor:help`, hover + `:focus-visible`), so it themes with the rest via shared tokens. To host a
non-nested control the card became an `<article>` with a **stretched title link** (whole card still
clicks through to the book); the `?` `<button>` sits above it, so clicking it never navigates. Cover
top-corners now round on the cover itself (card `overflow` opened up so the tooltip isn't clipped), and
a faint backdrop-blur keeps the badge legible over art. `npm test` green (5/5); `jekyll build` clean;
previewed in Chrome — verified the tooltip reveals on hover, the `?` doesn't navigate, and the rest of
the card does.

## 0.3.0 — Planted: *Every Lock but Hers* (a sixth book)

The first brand-new book since the starting shelf (cadence gate: 5 days since the last plant, ≥3
clears). **Every Lock but Hers** — a **novella** (~28,264-word cap) of magical realism, joining the
**Hollow Hours** universe and written by **Del Marsh**. Rolls, logged and honoured: form 0.87 →
novella; universe 0.95 → **join** an existing realm (the shelf is deepening, so lean-to-join);
author 0.93 → **reuse** an existing persona; sequel-lock 0.82 → not locked (a plain standalone).

The craft call behind the rolls: Del Marsh (the hard-boiled-tender caper voice) pointed at the
Hollow Hours' gentle magic is a deliberately cross-grain pairing — nothing like Posy Fell's
whimsical *Two-O'Clock Launderette* short, and nothing like Marsh's own Gaslight-Reach caper — and
it takes the thinnest universe (one small short) up to a real, tonally different pair. Nell
Corrigan is a night locksmith who can open any door in the city and has bolted the one she shut on
her brother; in the hollow hour a lock begins opening onto the places she's refused to go, for the
price of one truth she can't afford. One conflict, one change (locking-out → letting-in) — a
novella's engine. The **complete public blueprint** ships alongside: the magic's exact rule, the
wound, all eleven chapters, and who's behind the last door. **Chapter 1** (*The Two O'Clock Call*)
is written in full to prove the voice; ch. 2–11 are the daily grow job's to fill. Two new character
pages (Nell + Theo Corrigan) mesh in. Cover uses the designed title-fallback (art deferred,
budget-aware, as with grow chapters). `npm test` green (5 checks); `jekyll build` clean; shelf card,
book landing, blueprint, and the chapter reader all verified in the built output (byline resolves to
Del Marsh, 1/11 progress, Hollow Hours tag, counts → Books 6).
