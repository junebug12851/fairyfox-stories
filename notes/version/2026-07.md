## 0.3.2 — Shelf cards: loglines tuck behind a "?" corner tooltip

Adopted the fairyfox hub's project-card pattern on the story shelf: each book's logline no longer sits
as a always-on paragraph in the card body — it moves into a small **"?"** control in the cover's
top-right corner that reveals the line as a hover/focus tooltip. Cards read cleaner and scan faster
(cover, title, byline, progress — the pitch on demand), and the shelf now matches the hub's chrome
one more notch. Mechanics mirror the hub verbatim (`.proj-help` / `.proj-help-mark` / `.proj-tip`,
`cursor:help`, hover + `:focus-visible`), so it themes with the rest via shared tokens. To host a
non-nested control the card became an `<article>` with a **stretched title link** (whole card still
clicks through to the book); the `?` `<button>` sits above it, so clicking it never navigates. Cover
top-corners now round on the cover itself (card `overflow` opened up so the tooltip isn't clipped), and
a faint backdrop-blur keeps the badge legible over art. `npm test` green (5/5); `jekyll build` clean;
previewed in Chrome — verified the tooltip reveals on hover, the `?` doesn't navigate, and the rest of
the card does.

## 0.3.0 — Planted: *Every Lock but Hers* (a sixth book)

The first brand-new book since the starting shelf (cadence gate: 5 days since the last plant, ≥3
clears). **Every Lock but Hers** — a **novella** (~28,264-word cap) of magical realism, joining the
**Hollow Hours** universe and written by **Del Marsh**. Rolls, logged and honoured: form 0.87 →
novella; universe 0.95 → **join** an existing realm (the shelf is deepening, so lean-to-join);
author 0.93 → **reuse** an existing persona; sequel-lock 0.82 → not locked (a plain standalone).

The craft call behind the rolls: Del Marsh (the hard-boiled-tender caper voice) pointed at the
Hollow Hours' gentle magic is a deliberately cross-grain pairing — nothing like Posy Fell's
whimsical *Two-O'Clock Launderette* short, and nothing like Marsh's own Gaslight-Reach caper — and
it takes the thinnest universe (one small short) up to a real, tonally different pair. Nell
Corrigan is a night locksmith who can open any door in the city and has bolted the one she shut on
her brother; in the hollow hour a lock begins opening onto the places she's refused to go, for the
price of one truth she can't afford. One conflict, one change (locking-out → letting-in) — a
novella's engine. The **complete public blueprint** ships alongside: the magic's exact rule, the
wound, all eleven chapters, and who's behind the last door. **Chapter 1** (*The Two O'Clock Call*)
is written in full to prove the voice; ch. 2–11 are the daily grow job's to fill. Two new character
pages (Nell + Theo Corrigan) mesh in. Cover uses the designed title-fallback (art deferred,
budget-aware, as with grow chapters). `npm test` green (5 checks); `jekyll build` clean; shelf card,
book landing, blueprint, and the chapter reader all verified in the built output (byline resolves to
Del Marsh, 1/11 progress, Hollow Hours tag, counts → Books 6).

## 0.1.0 — Bootstrap the story farm (greenfield)

Set Fairy Fox Stories up from scratch as an AI story farm, per the hub `new-project-setup`
runbook (collection/monorepo exception, greenfield after clearing a prior unauthorized
scaffold). Adopted the hub standards + living-notes system. Wrote the **craft body of

(`craft/style-spectrum.md`, `craft/author-personas.md`) so every book rolls its own prose style
and a distinct passionate "author" — the shelf reads like a collaboration of many writers. Made
**pseudo-authors a first-class, meshed collection** (`_authors`): planting rolls new-vs-existing
author (like the universe roll), books carry an `author` byline linking to the author's page, and
author pages auto-list their body of work — a mesh even between pseudo-authors. First author:
**Del Marsh** (the wry street-poet behind *The Cinderwick Job*).
