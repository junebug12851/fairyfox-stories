The *Ark Meridian* has been under way for four hundred and eleven years. It is a city the size of a
mountain, sealed and turning, carrying the great-great-grandchildren of people who chose to leave a
world none of the living have seen, bound for one none of the living will reach. Everyone aboard is
born to the voyage and will die on it; only the ship remembers both ends of the journey, and the
ship does not gossip.

Life is orderly, close, and quiet — the long quiet of people who have made peace with a horizon that
is always exactly the same distance away. There are farms in the light-decks and cold in the
outer ones. There are charts of every corridor. There is the Purpose, taught to every child: *we
carry the world forward.* And there are, if you go looking, small discrepancies — decks that appear
on no map, corridors longer inside than out, records that don't quite add up — which most people are
sensible enough not to go looking for.

## Ground rules

- **Hard-ish, human-scaled SF.** No aliens, no faster-than-light miracles. The wonder is scale,
  time, sealed ecology, and the slow mystery of a machine older than anyone's memory.
- **The ship keeps secrets structurally** — in its architecture, its records, its silences. Mystery
  is discovery, not the supernatural.
- **Time is the antagonist and the marvel** — generations, drift, the weight of a purpose no one
  chose.

## Texture

Recycled air and grow-lamp violet. The hum of the turning hull. Numbered decks, laminated charts,
the smell of the farm-decks and the cold of the dark ones. Ritual, rationing, and routine.

## Recurring forces

- **The Purpose** — the ship's founding mission, taught as gospel; the thing a curious mind starts
  to doubt.
- **The Cartographers' Office** — keepers of the ship's maps, and therefore of what's official.

## Seeds

- A cartographer who finds a deck that isn't on any chart.
- The generation that discovers the ship slowed down a century ago and no one was told.
- A recording of the founders, meant never to be played.
