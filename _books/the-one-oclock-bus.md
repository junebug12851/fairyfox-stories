A novella of the Hollow Hours — the second tale in the realm of *The Two-O'Clock Launderette*, and
a story that stands entirely on its own. Abel Mensah drives the Route 9 night service exactly as
printed, except for the hour when it can't be, and keeps two records of the difference: the
official log, which lies beautifully, and a blue notebook, which doesn't. Eleven years of minutes
that never balance have finally been noticed. The auditor believes a fudged log is always
somebody's cover. She's right — she's just wrong about whose.

_First person, past tense; cool and exact, an orderly man reporting the one disorderly hour of his
night. Ten chapters. The full plan — audit, override, and front door — is in the
[blueprint](blueprint/)._
