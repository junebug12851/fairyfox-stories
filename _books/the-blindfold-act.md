A mystery of the Sawdust Circuit. Madame Sonora reads strangers for a dime — the tan line, the held
hat, the lie behind the smile — and has spent fifteen years being unreadable herself. Then Colonel
Aurelio is found dead in his locked pay-wagon with the week's take gone, the law wants it pinned on
the show, and the show wants it buried by Friday. Sonora starts reading her own family to keep them
safe — and reads, in the one face she never wanted to, exactly what happened.

_First person, past tense; wry, confessional, voice-forward. Six chapters. The whole case, the turn,
and the ending are laid out in the [blueprint](blueprint/) — a **standalone**, marked complete with
no sequel._
