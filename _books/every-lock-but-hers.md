A novella of the Hollow Hours. Nell Corrigan works the two o'clock calls — locked-out drunks,
snapped keys, the ugly closer jobs nobody else will take. She can open anything. She just can't
open the one door she shut herself, three years ago, on her brother.

Then, in the hollow hour, a lock she's picking swings open onto somewhere it has no business
reaching — and keeps offering her the way back. All it wants is the truth she's kept bolted.

_First person, wry and quick, a soft spot under the banter. Eleven chapters. The whole plan —
including who's behind the last door — is in the [blueprint](blueprint/)._
