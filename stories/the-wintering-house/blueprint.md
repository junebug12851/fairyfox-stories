**Form:** novella (~21,000-word cap) · **Genre:** gothic · **POV:** first person, past tense ·
**Universe:** Blackmoor · **Status:** growing. The whole plan, and the secret, laid open.

## Why a novella

One house, one companion, one buried secret, deepening — the novella's concentration: a single
dread developed slowly across eleven chapters, no wandering subplot, a near-single-sitting unitary
chill.

## Premise

Agnes Thorne is hired to be companion to Mrs Ravenscar of Wintering House — but Mrs Ravenscar is
never at home, never seen, and yet her room is kept warm, her place laid, her linens changed. The
servants will not say her name. Agnes, hungry to belong, is tempted to ask no questions. The novella
is the slow, patient uncovering of what the family is keeping warm — and Agnes's choice between a
comfortable belonging built on the lie, and the cold truth.

## The arc (want vs. need)

Agnes **wants** a place, a family, to be kept. She **needs** to refuse a comfortable lie even at the
cost of belonging. Wintering House offers her exactly the belonging she craves — on the condition
that she never truly look.

## The shape (eleven chapters, briefly)

1. **The Arrival.** Agnes reaches Wintering House; the cold house, the one warm room, the missing
   mistress, the servants' silence. *(written)*
2–4. **The Comfortable Lie.** Agnes settles, is welcomed, is *wanted* — and trains herself not to
   notice the warm room, the extra place at table, the portrait added to.
5–7. **The Cracks.** Small wrongnesses accumulate: a letter in a dead woman's hand, a name spoken and
   snatched back, the warm room's fire that no servant admits to laying.
8–9. **The Secret.** Agnes learns the truth: Mrs Ravenscar died years ago, and the family keeps her
   room, her place, her fiction, alive — a shared refusal to grieve so total it has become the
   household's whole life, and they mean Agnes to join it, to be the next who tends the warmth and
   never asks.
10. **The Offer.** They offer Agnes what she has always wanted — to belong, to be family, to be kept
    — for the price of keeping the lie warm forever.
11. **Cold and Clear.** Agnes chooses. She grieves the woman the family would not, opens the warm
    room's window to the moor wind, and leaves the belonging behind — keeping, instead, herself.

## Ending

No vengeful ghost — the haunting was grief that refused to end, a family that mistook keeping a room
warm for keeping a person alive. Agnes's triumph is the hard, lonely one: to see clearly, to mourn
honestly, and to walk out of the comfortable lie into the cold. Sad, eerie, earned. A standalone.

## Content note

Sustained gothic dread; an inherited secret about a death and denied grief. Unsettling and sad;
no gore, nothing graphic.
